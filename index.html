ERLANG B FORMULA

Erlang B is a model used to estimate the probability of call blocking in a system where calls arrive and require a certain amount of resources (like a line or server), and there are no waiting queues. If all the available lines are in use, the call is blocked, and the probability of blocking is calculated using the Erlang B formula.



P_B = Blocking probability (i.e., the probability that a call will be blocked because there are no available servers/lines).
A = Traffic intensity (or offered traffic), in Erlangs. This represents the average call load.
N = Number of servers or lines available.



Concept of Erlang B:

The traffic intensity (A) is a measure of the demand on the system, representing how many calls are being made and how long they last. N is the number of available lines or servers to handle the calls. If all lines are occupied, incoming calls are blocked, and P_B represents the likelihood of this happening.



Erlang B is used in systems where calls are blocked when all servers are busy and there's no possibility of queuing the call for later processing (e.g., voice calls).
